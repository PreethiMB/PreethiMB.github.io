About me

Mapping the living world

I'm a geospatial analyst focused on conservation and carbon monitoring — using remote sensing, spatial analysis, and field data to understand ecosystems at scale.

My work spans forest biomass estimation, wetland carbon dynamics, habitat connectivity modelling, and disturbance analysis. I'm particularly interested in California's diverse ecosystems and how geospatial tools can support both carbon markets and conservation planning.

Currently building expertise in blue carbon systems and the intersection of regulatory frameworks (CARB) with remote sensing verification.

Google Earth Engine QGIS / ArcGIS Python R LiDAR SAR / InSAR Carbon accounting Habitat modelling NDVI / vegetation indices Spatial statistics
